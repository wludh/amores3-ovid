About

How to Use This Site

This site is designed to provide a dynamic and interactive experience for exploring Ovid's Amores. The main page is divided into three primary sections, or "panels," that work together to present the text, manuscript images, and scholarly commentary.

The Three-Panel Layout

The interface consists of three panels: Transcription, IIIF Viewer, and Companion.

  • Customizing Your View: You can resize each panel by clicking and dragging the vertical borders between them. You can also reorder the panels by dragging the "☰" icon at the top-left of each panel. To collapse a panel, click the "×" icon; click it again to expand.

Exploring the Text
  1. Select a Poem: In the Transcription panel, use the "Select a poem..." dropdown menu to choose which of the Amores you wish to examine.
  2. Choose a Witness: Below the poem selector, the "Witness" dropdown allows you to load the transcription from a specific manuscript source (e.g., P, Y, S, or LL).

Viewing the Manuscripts
  • In the IIIF Viewer panel, you can view high-resolution images of the manuscripts. Click the buttons labeled "Witness P," "Witness Y," or "Witness S" to load the corresponding manuscript.
  • The viewer is synchronized with the poem selection. When you choose a new poem from the dropdown in the Transcription panel, the IIIF viewer will automatically jump to the page where that poem begins in the selected manuscript.
  • Standard controls for paging through the manuscript and zooming in on the images are available at the bottom of the viewer.

Accessing the Commentary
  • The Companion panel provides contextual information. Check the boxes for "Commentary," "Text Commentary," or "Vocabulary" to display the desired notes for the selected poem.

Interactive Linking

The Transcription and Companion panels are linked to enhance your reading experience:

  • Hovering: Hover your mouse over a line in the transcription to highlight the corresponding notes in the companion panel, and vice-versa.
  • Clicking: Click on a line in the transcription to automatically scroll the companion panel to the relevant notes. Similarly, clicking on a note in the companion will scroll the transcription to the corresponding line.

Dark Mode

For a different viewing experience, you can toggle dark mode by clicking the theme toggle icon in the header navigation.
